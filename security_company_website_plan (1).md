# Website Plan: Threat Assessment & Site Security Services

## 1. Project Overview

This website will present a professional security consulting and threat assessment service connected to the credibility and operating history of **Virtual Doorman**. The new site should clearly explain that the company helps property owners, managers, boards, commercial operators, and residential communities identify security vulnerabilities before they become incidents.

The parent company, Virtual Doorman, is positioned around building security, access control, remote operators, visitor management, deliveries, vendor access, resident support, and centralized oversight. Virtual Doorman describes its service as a building-wide system integrating video intercom, CCTV, and access control, with trained operators remotely monitoring properties and controlling access. This new website should extend that trust into a higher-touch advisory and inspection service: **professional onsite threat assessments and security evaluations**.

## 3. Business Goals for the Website

The website should be designed to accomplish five core goals:

1. Generate qualified leads from property managers, building owners, boards, developers, and commercial operators.
2. Explain the value of a professional onsite threat assessment in simple, non-alarmist language.
3. Connect the new service to Virtual Doorman’s established expertise in remote doorman, access control, visitor verification, delivery coordination, and building security operations.
4. Build trust through clear process, credentials, practical deliverables, and strong calls to action.
5. Convert visitors into consultation requests, phone calls, or scheduled assessments.

# Services Page

## Purpose

Explain each service in detail and make it easy for a potential client to understand what they are buying.

## Recommended services

### 1. Onsite Threat Assessment

A professional security specialist visits the property and evaluates visible threats, vulnerabilities, and procedural weaknesses. This assessment is designed for property managers, owners, boards, and operators who want a clear understanding of current risks.

Possible evaluation areas:

- Main entrances
- Secondary entrances
- Side doors and service doors
- Lobby and concierge areas
- Loading docks
- Parking areas
- Package rooms
- Mailrooms
- Elevators
- Stairwells
- Roof access
- Mechanical rooms
- Amenity areas
- Perimeter and exterior access points

### 2. Security Operations Review

This review focuses on how people move through the property and how security procedures are actually followed.

Evaluation areas:

- Visitor access
- Vendor access
- Contractor access
- Delivery handling
- Resident/tenant lockouts
- Staff procedures
- Incident response procedures
- Key, fob, and credential management
- After-hours access
- Tailgating / piggybacking risks

### 3. Technology & Access-Control Review

This review identifies whether current technology supports the property’s security needs.

Evaluation areas:

- Intercoms
- CCTV cameras
- Access-control systems
- Door strikes and magnetic locks
- Smart locks
- Key fob systems
- Elevator controls
- Package room technology
- Remote monitoring opportunities
- Integration opportunities with Virtual Doorman services

### 4. Written Security Findings Report

After the assessment, the client receives a practical report that may include:

- Executive summary
- Property overview
- Identified vulnerabilities
- Risk priority levels
- Photos or location notes, if applicable
- Recommended improvements
- Short-term actions
- Long-term improvements
- Technology recommendations
- Operational recommendations
- Optional implementation roadmap

### 5. Follow-Up Consultation

A follow-up meeting allows the client to review the findings, ask questions, and determine the next steps.

# How It Works Page

## Recommended process

### Step 1: Initial Consultation

We discuss your property, current security concerns, existing systems, known incidents, and operational goals.

### Step 2: Onsite Site Visit

A security specialist conducts a walkthrough of the property, reviewing entrances, access points, procedures, technology, and areas of concern.

### Step 3: Risk Identification

We identify vulnerabilities related to physical access, visitor management, vendor access, delivery procedures, blind spots, staffing gaps, technology limitations, and daily operations.

### Step 4: Written Report

You receive a clear report with findings, risk priorities, and recommended improvements.

### Step 5: Action Plan Review

We review the report with your team and help determine next steps, which may include procedural changes, technology upgrades, Virtual Doorman integration, or additional security services.

# FAQ Page

## Recommended FAQs

### What is a threat assessment?

A threat assessment is a structured review of a property’s physical security, access points, procedures, technology, and operating risks. The goal is to identify vulnerabilities and recommend practical improvements.

### Do you provide onsite visits?

Yes. The core service includes an onsite visit to walk the property, review access points, observe procedures, and identify areas of concern.

### What types of properties do you assess?

We assess residential buildings, commercial properties, mixed-use buildings, apartment communities, condominiums, co-ops, office buildings, and other facilities.

### Will we receive a written report?

Yes. After the assessment, you receive a written report with findings, risk priorities, and recommended next steps.

### Can you recommend security technology?

Yes. Recommendations may include access control, intercoms, cameras, smart locks, lighting, remote monitoring, and other security-related improvements.

### Is this only for Virtual Doorman customers?

No. The service can support both existing Virtual Doorman clients and new clients who want a professional security assessment.

### Can this help us decide whether we need Virtual Doorman?

Yes. The assessment can identify whether remote doorman, access control, visitor verification, delivery coordination, or monitoring services would strengthen your property’s security operations.

### How long does an assessment take?

Timing depends on the size and complexity of the property. A small property may require a shorter walkthrough, while larger or multi-building properties may require a more detailed review.
